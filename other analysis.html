Total Crashes by Age Group and Gender

Young adults within the age range of 20-34 years were most involved in crashes, with males accounting for 72% of incidents. Fewer incidents were recorded among the elderly, 65 years and above.

Total Crashes by Weather

Adverse weather conditions, such as rain and snow, significantly contributed to crashes, while clear weather had the highest incident rates due to more driving activity.

Total Crashes by Traffic Controls and Driver Factor Conditions

Traffic signal violations and lane departures were major contributors to crashes, with distracted driving as a leading driver factor.

Total Crashes by Light Conditions

Crashes were more frequent during nighttime, particularly in areas with poor lighting. Daytime crashes were attributed to high traffic volumes.

Total Crashes by Speed Limit

Higher speed limits were associated with more severe crashes, while urban areas with lower speed limits had a higher frequency of minor collisions.
